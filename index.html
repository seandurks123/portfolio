My Projects

Canva & NYT Usability Research

Conducted user experience design research for Canva and The New York Times, focusing on improving digital platform accessibility, usability, and user engagement. This involved analyzing user behavior, identifying pain points, and providing actionable design recommendations to enhance overall user satisfaction. Through usability testing, surveys, and data analysis, I contributed to refining the user interface and optimizing key interactions to create a more intuitive and seamless experience.

Homework Tracker App

I designed and developed a mobile homework tracking application in Figma, focusing on usability and intuitive task management. Through multiple iterations, I created wireframes and interactive prototypes to refine the app’s layout, navigation, and functionality. User feedback played a key role in shaping the design, leading to improvements in task organization, calendar integration, and notification reminders. By testing and iterating on different design elements, I ensured a seamless and engaging user experience, making the final prototype both functional and visually appealing.

SnugLife

SnugLife is an ongoing project where I am continuously refining the brand and its marketing strategy. While I have conducted market research, developed branding, and outlined a digital strategy, I am still working on finalizing product designs, expanding consumer outreach, and exploring potential sales channels. As I continue to test different marketing approaches and gather feedback, my goal is to create a strong, recognizable brand that resonates with college students and builds a loyal customer base.
